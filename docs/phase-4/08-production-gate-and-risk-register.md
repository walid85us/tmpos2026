# 08 — Production Gate & Risk Register

**Scope:** the single authoritative register of gates that must clear before production go-live. Every gate has an owner (role), milestone, required evidence, a pass/fail rule, current state, and blocking severity. **Go-live is authorized separately by the owner after M9**, only when every blocking gate is `PASS`.

**Severity:** `BLOCKER` (go-live cannot proceed), `HIGH` (must be resolved or explicitly owner-waived), `MED` (resolve or accept with documented risk).
**Current state:** `OPEN` (not started/in progress), `PARTIAL`, `PASS`.

## Summary

- **Total gates:** 37. **BLOCKER:** 21 · **HIGH:** 14 · **MED:** 2. — unchanged since M1. **Lockfile portability is a mandatory subcriterion of G-APPSEC, not a new gate:** G-APPSEC already owns the dependency (SCA) audit, and that audit reads `package-lock.json`, so lock fidelity is a precondition of G-APPSEC's own pass rule rather than a separate risk. No existing gate had claimed lock integrity, clean installation, or supply-chain reproducibility, and none needed to be stretched to accept it. The pre-install checker is enforced regardless of this accounting; only the governance ownership is consolidated. Its non-waivability is a **rule**, not an assertion — see the non-waivable carve-out below.
- **Current state:** all `OPEN` at M1 (M1 is planning; it opens the register, it does not close gates). M2 moves G-EMU, G-APPSEC and G-COVERAGE to `PARTIAL`. **Phase 4.0 M3 (Shipping sidecar elimination) additionally moves G-SSRF and G-SECRETS to `PARTIAL`** — each had a distinct M3 half (eliminate the request-controlled fetch; remove the in-memory unauthenticated credential store) that is now done with regression evidence, while the remainder (M7e/M8 egress boundary; M6/M8 encrypted rotatable secrets) stays open. **G-UNAUTH remains `OPEN`, not `PARTIAL`**: the Shipping half is gone, but `POST /identity/resolve` is still unauthenticated and the shared enforced middleware does not exist. **The gate total and severity mix are unchanged (37 / 21 BLOCKER / 14 HIGH / 2 MED) — no gate was added, removed, downgraded, or closed.** **No gate is `PASS`.**
- **M0 carry-forward gates still open:** G-EMU, G-HIST, G-4USER.
- **M2 remains OPEN.** The lockfile recovery is verified locally only: the portability gate, the empty-cache clean install, and the typecheck contract have not yet been proven on a CI runner, which is the environment whose absence of the internal mirror caused the original failure. No M2 gate may be marked `PASS` on local evidence.
- **Go-live rule:** every `BLOCKER` must be `PASS` and every `HIGH` must be `PASS` or explicitly owner-waived (with documented risk) before G-GOLIVE can be requested.
- **Security-HIGH waiver rule:** waiving any **security** HIGH gate (e.g. G-APPSEC, G-WEBHARDEN, G-PROVIDER) requires, in addition to owner sign-off, (a) **Security sign-off**, (b) a **documented compensating control**, and (c) a **time-boxed remediation date**. This is why the previously-waivable security-integrity gates (webhook signature verification, the hardcoded PIN, the RLS-bypassing DB role, money-path idempotency) were **elevated to BLOCKER** rather than left owner-waivable.
- **Non-waivable carve-out — G-APPSEC's lockfile-portability subcriterion.** A G-APPSEC waiver may cover SAST/DAST/SCA **findings** (e.g. the four open transitive Highs). It may **never** waive the pre-install lockfile-portability checker, which is **non-waivable and blocks merge independently of G-APPSEC's disposition**. Without this carve-out a subcriterion silently inherits its parent's waivability, and a waiver granted over an unrelated advisory would dissolve supply-chain lock integrity along with it — which nobody waiving over `ws` intends. The carve-out is required because the usual "it would fail anyway" reasoning is **false here**: an unportable lockfile does **not** reliably break the build — npm *skips* an unfetchable `optional`/`devOptional` package and `npm ci` still exits 0. That silent path is the realised failure this gate exists to prevent (a green install that omitted `@types/react`), so the control cannot rest on the build failing loudly. Per the precedent above, a security-integrity control that must not be waivable is treated as blocking regardless of its host gate's severity.
- **Legal-HIGH waiver rule:** **G-PRIVACY is a statutory gate** (GDPR Arts 15–22 DSAR, CCPA/CPRA) — its absence at go-live is a legal violation, not a discretionary risk. Waiving G-PRIVACY (or its core export/deletion sub-scope) requires, beyond owner sign-off, (a) **legal-owner sign-off**, (b) a **documented lawful basis**, and (c) a **time-boxed remediation date** — an owner alone may not waive it. The core DSAR-fulfilment sub-scope is treated as effectively blocking for any deployment processing EU/CA-resident PII.
- **Severity semantics:** a gate's blocking severity may **exceed** the defect severity of its source gap (defect severity ≠ go-live-blocking severity). Examples: GAP-18 (defect High) → G-EMU (BLOCKER); GAP-24 (defect Medium) → G-HIST (HIGH). This is intentional, not an error.
- **Operational residual — carrier-credential rotation (owner action, not a code gate).** The eliminated Shipping sidecar accepted carrier credentials over unauthenticated routes and held them in plaintext process memory, and the preview surface that reached it was network-reachable. **This repository holds no evidence that any real carrier credential was ever entered, and none is asserted.** The residual is strictly conditional: **if** a real carrier credential was entered into the provider-settings surface at any time while the legacy preview/sidecar was reachable, **then** before production the store owner must (a) rotate that credential at the carrier/aggregator, and (b) review that provider account's billing and activity for the exposure window. The condition is **not decidable from repository evidence** — absence of historical misuse cannot be proven from this codebase, because the sidecar persisted no credential audit trail and its process memory is not recoverable. Accordingly this is an **owner operational production gate**, discharged by owner attestation, not by any test, scan, or code artifact in this repository; no local gate can close it and none claims to. **No email address, provider account, key fragment, credential identifier, or secret is recorded here, and none may be added.** Related code-side state: G-SECRETS is `PARTIAL` (the in-memory store is gone; encrypted-at-rest and rotatable durable secrets remain M6/M8).
- **Owner decisions RESOLVED (no open M1 owner decision remains):** **G-PCI — integrated payment capability is IN SCOPE** (semi-integrated/tokenized/provider-hosted; no raw cardholder data in TM POS2026; provider selected in M7b; PCI category validated before production). **G-PRIVACY — privacy & DSAR foundation is IN SCOPE** (implement, not exclude; distributed M1/M5/M6/M7/M8/M9). Both directions are **DECIDED**; both remain **evidence-based production gates** until their evidence passes.
